Troubleshooting

This section will be dedicated to identifying issues and giving tips to help troubleshoot them.

The most common issue when multi-color/multi-material printing is the temperature you are printing at. I have outlined a table that will give you a general range of the temperatures at which each type of filament will need to be printed. The temperature ranges will vary slightly with different filament manufacturers, but the table should give you a rough idea of where to start.

If you notice a popping noise coming from your extruder that sounds like the audio below, you may have a temperature issue. This means that either the extruder is not fully engaged into the plastic filament, or your temperature is not high enough to melt the amount of filament you are pushing into the hotend.

Materials Temperature Ranges
PLA 190˚C - 220˚C
ABS 230˚C - 240˚C
PETG 230˚C - 250˚C
TPU 210˚C - 225˚C
